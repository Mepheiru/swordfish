Signal Control -- How swordfish sends signals -- Compiled for v1.2.2

Swordfish gives the user extensive control over the signals sent to procceses. Bellow are some arguments that effect how swordfish sends signals.

  -k                     Send SIGTERM to matching processes (Graceful shutdown)
  -K                     Send SIGKILL to matching processes (Forceful shutdown)

The signals "-k" and "-K" act as soft and hard kill respectivly.
Signals other then KILL/TERM can be sent by using "-<sig>". For example: "-10" would send USR1 to the procces.
As stated in General Operation, flags can be grouped together. If "-k" and "-K" where grouped together (-kK) the arugment parser will use the last argument (K).
